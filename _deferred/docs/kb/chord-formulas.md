# Chord Formulas

## Triad Formulas (Scale Degrees)

Triads are built by stacking thirds from a scale:

- **Major triad**: 1, 3, 5 (root, major 3rd, perfect 5th)
- **Minor triad**: 1, ♭3, 5 (root, minor 3rd, perfect 5th)
- **Diminished triad**: 1, ♭3, ♭5 (root, minor 3rd, diminished 5th)
- **Augmented triad**: 1, 3, ♯5 (root, major 3rd, augmented 5th)

## Triad Formulas (Semitones from Root)

| Quality | Intervals | Semitones |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Major | M3 + m3 | 4 + 3 |
| Minor | m3 + M3 | 3 + 4 |
| Diminished | m3 + m3 | 3 + 3 |
| Augmented | M3 + M3 | 4 + 4 |

## Seventh Chord Formulas

| Quality | Structure | Semitones from root |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| Major 7th (maj7) | Major triad + M7 | 4, 3, 4 |
| Minor 7th (min7) | Minor triad + m7 | 3, 4, 3 |
| Dominant 7th (dom7) | Major triad + m7 | 4, 3, 3 |
| Half-diminished (ø7, m7♭5) | Dim triad + M7 | 3, 3, 4 |
| Diminished 7th (dim7) | Dim triad + dim7 | 3, 3, 3 |

## Diatonic Chords

Diatonic chords use only notes from the scale. In C major:

- I = C E G
- ii = D F A
- iii = E G B
- IV = F A C
- V = G B D
- vi = A C E
- vii° = B D F
