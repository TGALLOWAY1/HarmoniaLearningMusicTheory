# Scale Interval Patterns

## Major Scale

The major scale follows the interval pattern: **W–W–H–W–W–W–H**

- W = whole step = 2 semitones
- H = half step = 1 semitone

In semitones: **2, 2, 1, 2, 2, 2, 1**

Example: C major = C D E F G A B

| Degree | Note | Interval from root |
|--------|------|--------------------|
| 1 | C | 0 |
| 2 | D | 2 |
| 3 | E | 4 |
| 4 | F | 5 |
| 5 | G | 7 |
| 6 | A | 9 |
| 7 | B | 11 |

## Natural Minor Scale

The natural minor scale: **W–H–W–W–H–W–W**

In semitones: **2, 1, 2, 2, 1, 2, 2**

Example: A natural minor = A B C D E F G

| Degree | Note | Interval from root |
|--------|------|--------------------|
| 1 | A | 0 |
| 2 | B | 2 |
| 3 | C | 3 |
| 4 | D | 5 |
| 5 | E | 7 |
| 6 | F | 8 |
| 7 | G | 10 |

## Relative Major and Minor

Natural minor shares the same key signature as its relative major, a minor 3rd above:

- A minor ↔ C major
- E minor ↔ G major
- B minor ↔ D major
