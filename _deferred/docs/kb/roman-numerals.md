# Roman Numerals in Diatonic Harmony

## Overview

Roman numerals label chords by their scale degree and quality. Uppercase = major triad, lowercase = minor, ° = diminished.

## Major Key Diatonic Triads

| Degree | Chord | Quality |
|--------|-------|---------|
| I | I | major |
| ii | ii | minor |
| iii | iii | minor |
| IV | IV | major |
| V | V | major |
| vi | vi | minor |
| vii° | vii° | diminished |

## Major Key Diatonic Sevenths

| Degree | Chord | Quality |
|--------|-------|---------|
| I | Imaj7 | major 7th |
| ii | iimin7 | minor 7th |
| iii | iiimin7 | minor 7th |
| IV | IVmaj7 | major 7th |
| V | V7 | dominant 7th |
| vi | vimin7 | minor 7th |
| vii° | viiø7 | half-diminished 7th |

| Degree | Chord | Quality |
|--------|-------|---------|
| i | im7 | minor 7th |
| ii° | iiø7 | half-diminished 7th |
| III | IIImaj7 | major 7th |

| v | vm7 | minor 7th |
| VI | VImaj7 | major 7th |
| VII | VII7 | dominant 7th |

## Note on Harmonic Minor

Harmonic minor raises the 7th scale degree (e.g., G→G# in A minor). This creates a major V chord and diminished vii° triad. The MVP uses natural minor; harmonic minor is a common alteration for stronger dominant resolution.
